 Hello veterans and families, video number 69 from helping hands for ECHS focused on ECHS services. This video is an advisory which is released by Central Organization ECHS which is quite beneficiary friendly. Based on the feedback to Central Organization from various beneficiaries which is the feedback that patients have been referred to an M-panel hospital first for a particular investigation and then a referral for consultation. An referral for treatment and possibly finally a referral for admission. What is the effect of this is the beneficiary or the next of kin of the patient makes multiple trips from residents to polycline at the first instance from that M-panel hospital back to polyclinics and then back to M-panel hospital because of all these possible scenarios. Obviously, this needs to be avoided to the extent feasible. So the advisory from Central Organization is as below. Number one, referrals should be given such that beneficiary is not required to obtain fresh referral for a particular treatment. The exception is unless some other medical problem is discovered later involving a different line of treatment, obviously for that a new referral is required. Number two, using the clinical acumen medical officer at polyclinic must include the relevant actions by the M-panel hospital in the first instance only. This advisory will possibly ensure beneficiaries do not make multiple trips. As you are aware from the 68 videos, this is a volunteer effort by me that is helping hands for ECHS.
